Jumil, chumil (from Nahuatl xomilli or xotlinilli), or chinche de monte, are the names given in Mexico to several species of edible hemiptera insects of the Pentatomidae family.
They measure a little less than one centimeter (females are larger than males). They are consumed mainly in the states of Morelos and Guerrero.
